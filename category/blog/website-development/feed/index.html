Traditional SEO—long focused on keywords and blue links—is now only part of the equation. As platforms like ChatGPT, Gemini, and Perplexity dominate user behavior, CMOs must develop an AI search content strategy that prioritizes visibility across generative engines.

This article outlines how content must evolve, what marketing leaders need to prioritize, and how to monitor results in a shifting search ecosystem.

How AI Search Works

Unlike traditional search, where results are pulled from static rankings, AI search platforms generate synthesized answers. Users ask natural questions, and AI tools respond with context-rich summaries—often without requiring a click.

The rise of AI-led platforms is redefining visibility. Your content must now be structured and clear enough to be cited and summarized, not just ranked.

Strategy Shift: From Ranking to Referencing

Search engines display links. Generative engines reference chunks of content.

As explained in this guide by Aprimo, “an AI-driven content strategy is a current imperative.” Instead of relying on high search positions, marketers must build trust signals that earn citations within AI-generated answers.

This means writing content that helps AI understand, not just humans read.

Key Components of an AI Search Content Strategy

1. Target Natural Language Queries

AI models thrive on question-based prompts. Unlike traditional keyword targeting, your content should mirror how real users ask for information.

Use subheadings like:

  • “What are the best strategies for optimizing content for generative AI?”
  • “How can CMOs adapt to AI-driven search?”

These match user behavior and AI query patterns.

2. Cover Intent Clusters, Not Just Topics

AI systems use latent intent analysis and query expansion (also called “query fan-out”). Your content should support multiple intent types:
Informational: “What is generative engine optimization?”

  • Comparative: “GEO vs traditional SEO”
  • Transactional: “How to get cited in AI answers”

Supporting diverse query intents improves your likelihood of being selected in AI summaries, as explained in this article from Aleyda Solis.

3. Structure Content for AI Readability

Formatting matters more than ever. Use:

  • Clear <h2> and <h3> headings
  • Bullet points and numbered lists
  • Short, factual paragraphs
  • Summaries at the top of the page

According to Harvard DCE, generative tools prioritize content that is “structured, summarized, and easy to parse.”

Schema markup like FAQPage, HowTo, and Article improves your visibility across Google’s AI Overviews and other generative platforms.

4. Demonstrate Authority, Not Just Keywords

AI models evaluate the trustworthiness of your brand, not just the frequency of phrases. They look for:

  • Verified data and credible citations
  • Brand mentions across external domains
  • Consistent author credentials and bios

As discussed in this article by Search Engine Land, co-citations and factual clarity outweigh keyword stuffing.

5. Keep Content Fresh

AI models value recency. Update pages to reflect the latest stats, guidelines, and shifts in the market. Use visible “Last updated” tags and refresh high-performing assets quarterly.

This matters not just for SEO, but for earning AI citations.

Monitoring Performance in AI Search

Metrics to Track

AI search doesn’t offer traditional ranking reports. Instead, monitor:

  • Citation frequency in tools like ChatGPT and Perplexity
  • Brand mentions in AI-generated answers
  • Changes in long-tail traffic and branded search volume
  • Time on page and scroll depth for cited content

Platforms like Semrush’s AI Toolkit help track AI market share and citation frequency.

What About Google Search Console?

As of June 2025, Google now includes AI Mode traffic in its Search Console. However, AI Mode components are tracked as separate positions, making analysis more complex. Impression and click data may not fully capture brand exposure within AI answers—another reason to monitor citations directly.

Challenges for CMOs

  • Clickless discovery is rising. Traditional performance metrics are less reliable. AI may mention your brand without generating traffic.
  • Internal processes lag behind. Most content teams aren’t structured for continuous refresh cycles or entity-based optimization.
  • Tooling is fragmented. No single platform tracks all AI visibility; CMOs must combine tools and qualitative monitoring.

But with these challenges come opportunities. According to Beeby Clark+Meyler, “content designed for AI is easier to scale, more evergreen, and often more effective at driving qualified engagement—even if fewer clicks are involved.”

Recommendations for CMOs

  1. Audit existing content for AI readiness. Look at structure, clarity, freshness, and topical completeness.
  2. Build content hubs around key entities and topics. This improves your chances of citation across queries.
  3. Assign responsibility for AI search visibility. Appoint someone to track mentions and coordinate updates.
  4. Collaborate with digital PR and social. Increase authoritative mentions that build brand trust and context for AI engines.
  5. Invest in scalable content refresh workflows. Prioritize pages that are already generating traffic or citations.

Conclusion

The way users search—and how content is surfaced—is changing rapidly. CMOs must respond by developing a content strategy that is AI-first, not just SEO-optimized.

Start with content that:

  • Answers real user questions
  • Is structured and skimmable
  • Demonstrates authority
  • Stays fresh
  • Aligns with the buying journey

]]> From Setback to Startup: How Asking the Hard Questions Fuels Entrepreneurial Growth https://nowspeed.com/blog/from-setback-to-startup-how-asking-the-hard-questions-fuels-entrepreneurial-growth/ Thu, 25 Sep 2025 13:00:27 +0000 https://nowspeed.com/?p=36909 At Nowspeed, we’ve worked with entrepreneurs across industries, and one truth always stands out: the most successful founders aren’t the ones who have all the answers, but the ones who know how to ask the right questions.

Entrepreneurship is rarely a straight path. Many great companies are born out of setbacks, detours, and unexpected opportunities. But what separates those who simply “get lucky” from those who build lasting success is preparation, persistence, and a willingness to reach out, learn, and take risks.

In this article, we’ll share key lessons that every business leader can apply when launching or growing a company.

Turning Setbacks Into Opportunities

Most entrepreneurs start with a plan. But the reality is, plans often change—sometimes on day one. Careers are derailed, industries shift, and entire opportunities disappear overnight. What matters most is how you respond when the unexpected happens.

Successful entrepreneurs don’t view setbacks as permanent failures. They see them as openings to pivot, learn, and adapt. That might mean taking a temporary role to pay the bills, using that time to make connections, or recognizing an opportunity in what at first feels like bad luck.

The key is to stay open and proactive. Every conversation, every chance encounter can spark the next chapter of your business journey—if you’re prepared to seize it.

Luck Favors the Prepared

It’s easy to look at a successful entrepreneur and say, “They were just lucky.” Right place, right time. Right connections. But luck alone doesn’t build companies.

What looks like luck is often the result of preparation meeting opportunity. Entrepreneurs who are willing to put themselves out there—ask questions, take risks, try new things—create the conditions for “luck” to happen.

In practice, this means showing up even when the odds are against you, asking questions even when you don’t know the answer, and having the courage to pursue an opportunity when it presents itself.

The Power of Asking Hard Questions

One of the most underrated skills in business is simply asking. Whether it’s reaching out to a potential mentor, pitching a customer, or negotiating with an investor, success often begins with a difficult question.

Many entrepreneurs hesitate to ask because they fear rejection. But nine times out of ten, starting a conversation leads somewhere productive. Even a “no” often comes with insights or connections that open the next door.

Asking the hard questions not only helps you find answers—it builds relationships. Customers, colleagues, and mentors all respond to curiosity and honesty. The willingness to learn often inspires others to help.

Building Businesses Around Problems, Not Products

Too many companies start with the assumption that they already know what the market wants. They fall in love with an idea, raise money, and launch without validating whether customers actually need or care about the solution.

A better approach is to start with a problem. Look around your community, your industry, or even your own life. Where do people struggle? What challenges keep coming up in conversation? Which problems are so persistent that people are willing to pay for solutions?

When you build a business around a clear, validated problem, you’re creating a foundation for long-term success. Your product or service becomes a tool that solves something real, rather than a “cool idea” looking for an audience.

Research Is Not Optional

The difference between a dreamer and a successful entrepreneur often comes down to research.

That means more than Googling statistics or reading articles. It means talking to people who have already done it. Reaching out on LinkedIn to industry veterans. Buying coffee for respected local leaders. Calling people in other regions who aren’t competitors and asking for advice.

The most valuable insights don’t come from reports—they come from conversations. Entrepreneurs who seek out mentorship, listen actively, and compile insights from multiple sources are far better equipped to write realistic business plans and avoid costly mistakes.

The Business Plan Still Matters

In today’s fast-moving startup world, business plans sometimes get dismissed as old-fashioned. But a solid business plan isn’t about writing a 50-page document for investors. It’s about testing your assumptions, projecting realistic scenarios, and clarifying the steps to move forward.

A good plan forces you to be honest with yourself:

  • How big is the opportunity?
  • What resources will you need?
  • What risks could derail you?
  • How will you measure success?

When combined with real-world research, a business plan becomes a living tool that guides decision-making and keeps you accountable.

Mentorship Is Fuel for Growth

Entrepreneurship can feel isolating, but it doesn’t have to be. The most effective entrepreneurs surround themselves with mentors, peers, and advisors who help them see blind spots and refine their ideas.

Mentorship doesn’t always come from formal programs. Sometimes it’s as simple as asking someone you admire to meet for coffee. Many successful leaders are eager to give back, especially when approached with genuine curiosity and respect.

Regular conversations with mentors help you learn from their mistakes, accelerate your decision-making, and gain the confidence to take bigger swings.

Learn by Doing

At some point, research and planning need to give way to action. Many entrepreneurs start small—buying a property, fixing it up, or testing a product on a limited scale.

This hands-on approach creates two advantages. First, it teaches practical skills. Second, it builds resilience. By rolling up your sleeves, you learn how to solve problems, manage costs, and adapt to surprises. Those lessons compound over time, giving you the confidence to take on larger and riskier ventures.

Entrepreneurship Is About Improving Lives

At its best, entrepreneurship isn’t just about profit. It’s about creating jobs, solving problems, and making communities stronger. Whether through real estate, technology, or services, entrepreneurs play a critical role in improving the lives of those around them.

This bigger purpose helps sustain entrepreneurs through setbacks and challenges. When you focus on the positive impact your business can make, the hard work feels more meaningful—and customers notice.

Conclusion: Success Comes From Curiosity and Courage

Every entrepreneur’s journey looks different. Some start with big ideas, others stumble into opportunities by accident. But the path to success always involves the same core traits: curiosity, persistence, and courage.

At Nowspeed, we’ve seen how the willingness to ask hard questions, seek out mentors, validate problems, and build realistic plans consistently leads to growth. Entrepreneurship isn’t about having all the answers—it’s about being willing to learn, adapt, and take risks when opportunity presents itself.

The next time you’re facing a setback, wondering if luck will ever break your way, or considering launching a new venture, remember: luck favors the prepared. And preparation begins with a question.

]]> Mastering Conversion Rate Optimization: Key Strategies for Success https://nowspeed.com/blog/mastering-conversion-rate-optimization-key-strategies-for-success/ Thu, 17 Oct 2024 17:36:41 +0000 https://nowspeed.com/?p=36429 Conversion Rate Optimization (CRO) is one of the most powerful strategies to improve your marketing efforts by turning more visitors into leads, subscribers, or customers. It’s not just about driving traffic to your website; it’s about ensuring that once people arrive, they are compelled to take action. In this blog, we’ll explore key strategies for optimizing conversion rates, how to test effectively, and the tools that can help you achieve your goals.

What Is Conversion Rate Optimization?

At its core, Conversion Rate Optimization (CRO) is the practice of increasing the percentage of website visitors who take a desired action, known as a “conversion.” This could be anything from filling out a form to making a purchase, downloading a guide, or signing up for an event.

CRO is essential for marketers because it helps maximize the value of your existing traffic. By improving your website’s efficiency, you can lower the cost of acquiring new customers, whether the traffic comes from organic search, paid ads, or social media. Ultimately, CRO is about making your marketing dollars work harder by driving more leads or sales without increasing the amount of traffic.

The Scientific Method for CRO

CRO is driven by a test-and-learn mindset. By treating each change as an experiment, you can make informed decisions about what works and what doesn’t. This process follows a simple but effective pattern:

  • Form a hypothesis: Identify what you believe will improve your conversion rate.
  • Run a test: Make the change on your website and collect data.
  • Analyze the results: See if the change had the intended impact.
  • Adjust and scale: If the test is successful, implement the change and continue testing new elements.

Testing removes guesswork from the equation and allows you to make data-driven decisions. This scientific approach can lead to significant improvements in your marketing campaigns.

Key Elements to Test for CRO

There are many factors that influence whether a visitor takes action on your website. Here are some of the most important elements to test and optimize:

1. Value Proposition

The value proposition is the first thing visitors should notice when they land on your page. It needs to be crystal clear and compelling. Answer the question: “Why should I care?” A strong value proposition highlights the unique benefits your product or service offers.

Use specific language, avoid jargon, and focus on the problem your solution solves. For instance, instead of saying, “Increase your marketing efficiency,” you could say, “Save 10 hours a week by automating your social media marketing.”

2. Formatting and Copy

Even the best value proposition can fall flat if it’s buried in a wall of text. Break up long paragraphs with bullet points, subheadings, and white space. Visitors should be able to scan your page quickly to understand what you offer.

Consider using bolded subheadings that outline key takeaways, benefits, or features. Simplifying the layout makes it easier for users to engage with your content and is a quick win for improving conversions.

3. Imagery

High-quality images help tell your story visually. Whether it’s showing your product in action or using infographics to simplify complex data, images can significantly enhance your message. Avoid generic stock photos; instead, use visuals that add credibility to your offer, such as product images, data visualizations, or testimonials.

4. Social Proof

Social proof is one of the most effective tools for building trust. Customer reviews, testimonials, case studies, and even statistics like “5,000 satisfied clients” can help sway potential leads to take action. Media mentions or partnerships with credible brands can also add a level of legitimacy to your offer.

5. Calls to Action (CTAs)

CTAs are critical to driving conversions. Experiment with different button texts, such as “Get Started,” “Claim Your Offer,” or “Transform Your Business Today.” You can also test the placement of CTAs—at the top of the page, in the middle, and at the end. Adding a sense of urgency, such as “Only 3 spots left,” can boost engagement.

6. Form Fields

When it comes to generating leads, simpler is better. The more fields you ask users to fill out, the more likely they are to abandon the form. Work with your sales team to determine the minimum amount of information needed to qualify leads. Avoid “phone number” as a mandatory field unless absolutely necessary, and keep forms as short as possible.

Tools to Measure CRO Success

Once you’ve made changes to your landing page or website, it’s time to measure the results. There are several tools that can help track conversions, user behavior, and engagement.

1. Google Analytics 4

Google Analytics is a staple for tracking user behavior. Key performance indicators (KPIs) like conversion rate, average cost per acquisition (CPA), and time on page give you insight into how well your page is performing. One crucial feature in Google Analytics 4 is “key events”, formerly known as conversions in GA4 which allow you to track important actions, like form fills or button clicks, and measure the impact of your changes.

2. Microsoft Clarity

Microsoft Clarity is an excellent (and free!) tool for CRO. It offers heatmaps, session recordings, and user behavior insights that allow you to see how visitors interact with your page in real-time. For example, if users are repeatedly clicking on a non-functional area of the page (known as “rage clicks”), you can identify and fix the issue quickly.

3. AI-Powered Insights

Clarity also includes AI-based insights, which can automatically analyze user behavior and provide recommendations for improvements. This can save time and give you a head start on identifying the most critical areas for optimization.

Conclusion

CRO is an essential part of any successful digital marketing strategy. By focusing on testing and refining key elements like value propositions, CTAs, form fields, and social proof, you can increase your conversion rate and get more value from your existing traffic.

Testing is the key to mastering CRO. By analyzing results and making incremental changes, you can continuously improve your landing pages and campaigns, driving better results with each iteration.

]]> How Can Innovation Set You Apart From Your Competitors? https://nowspeed.com/blog/how-can-innovation-set-you-apart-from-your-competitors/ Fri, 02 Feb 2024 16:42:27 +0000 https://nowspeed.com/?p=36047 Is a unique idea the key to success? It’s a common belief that to thrive in the competitive world of business, you need that one groundbreaking idea that will set you apart from the rest.

In this article, we’ll explore the concept of innovation as a driving force behind business success. While a groundbreaking idea can certainly be a game-changer, it’s not the only path to triumph in the business world. Let’s delve into why innovation is a powerful tool that can propel your business forward and how it can be harnessed to your advantage.

The Demystification of the Unique Idea

It’s not uncommon for aspiring entrepreneurs to believe that they must come up with a completely novel business concept to make their mark. Many people are stuck in the mindset that they need to reinvent the wheel, creating something entirely unheard of to succeed. However, this notion has been widely debunked by experienced entrepreneurs and business experts.

Mike Vulcan, a serial entrepreneur and author with a track record of building and selling six companies, highlights this point in his book, Business Success Formula. He emphasizes that you don’t necessarily need a brand-new, unique idea to succeed. In fact, he encourages entrepreneurs to consider existing proven business models that resonate with their expertise. Starting a business in a domain you’re familiar with, even if similar ventures already exist, can be a smart approach. You can put your unique twist on an existing concept and enter the market with a product or service that is already validated.

The First-Mover Advantage

While it’s true that being the first to market can offer a competitive edge, it doesn’t guarantee long-term success. First-mover advantage often diminishes as competitors enter the scene. Take ride-sharing services as an example. Uber was a pioneer in the industry, but it didn’t take long for Lyft to emerge as a strong competitor.

For many entrepreneurs, it can be more advantageous to enter an established market with a proven demand for a product or service. When you’re not introducing a completely new concept, potential customers already understand the value of what you’re offering. This means you can focus on building your brand and delivering exceptional value rather than educating the market.

The Role of Competitive Advantage

While innovation doesn’t always mean inventing a new product or service, having a competitive advantage can significantly impact your success. It’s essential to assess your competitors and identify areas where you can outperform them. This might involve improving upon existing solutions, offering better customer service, or finding a unique selling proposition.

However, it’s crucial to note that competing solely on price, by offering a cheaper alternative, may not be the most sustainable strategy. Lowering prices can lead to reduced profit margins, potential issues with company culture, and even long-term damage to your brand. Instead, focus on value-driven innovation, emphasizing how your product or service can save time, save money, or help your customers make more money.

The Business Success Formula

In the pursuit of business success, innovation plays a pivotal role. Mike Vulcan’s Business Success Formula breaks down the key components that contribute to a thriving business. While innovation is just one part of the formula, it’s a crucial element that shouldn’t be overlooked.

Vulcan’s formula includes Talent (1.5x weight), Performance, Brand, Innovation, and Money. The Talent aspect emphasizes the importance of assembling a capable team. Performance involves executing your business strategy effectively. Branding ensures your company’s identity and reputation are strong. Money, combined with Innovation, contributes to your financial success.

Cultivating a Culture of Innovation

For businesses aiming to foster innovation, the key lies in building a culture that encourages and supports it. Here are some actionable steps to cultivate a culture of innovation within your organization:

  1. Create Awareness: Make innovation a part of your company’s ethos. Communicate that innovation is not only welcomed but encouraged at all levels of the organization.
  2. Provide Channels: Establish channels for employees to submit innovative ideas. Consider anonymous suggestion boxes or digital platforms where ideas can be shared.
  3. Open Dialogue: Encourage open dialogue among employees. Host regular brainstorming sessions or meetings where team members can discuss and refine ideas collectively.
  4. Reward Innovation: Recognize and reward employees for their innovative contributions. This can be through monetary incentives, promotions, or acknowledgment in company-wide communications.
  5. Lead by Example: Leadership should set an example by embracing innovation themselves. When employees see their leaders actively engaging in innovative practices, it sets the tone for the entire organization.
  6. Implement Innovation Programs: Consider formalizing innovation programs or initiatives. Assign individuals or teams to oversee these efforts and ensure they are consistently applied.

Measuring the Impact of Innovation

While innovation itself may not always be quantifiable in the traditional sense, its effects can be measured indirectly. Key performance indicators (KPIs) such as increased revenue, customer satisfaction, employee engagement, and market share growth can all be indicative of a successful culture of innovation.

Moreover, the Business Success Formula by Mike Vulcan offers a structured approach to measuring and improving various aspects of your business, including innovation. By regularly assessing your organization’s performance, you can identify areas where innovation is making a difference and areas that may require more attention.

Conclusion

Innovation is undeniably a powerful tool for success in the business world.
